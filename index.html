The lending arm of Cars24 — and the fact that shapes this whole strategy is that it owns its NBFC, the marketplace, and the car's data, all at once.

What it is
RBI-registered NBFC (CARS24 Financial Services), on top of India's largest used-car marketplace.
Products
Used-car loans, loan-against-car, top-ups, MSME/personal loans, balance transfer.
B2C terms today
Up to 100% financing, ~10.99–12% p.a., tenure up to 6–7 yrs.

02

The lacuna we're attacking

A used-car NBFC's hardest problem isn't speed — it's the interest rate. The customer feels it directly:

"Why should I pay Cars24 ₹3,000 more a month than my bank?"
Banks · HDFC / SBI
9.5–10.5%
Cheapest capital — but slow, document-heavy, prime-only
NBFCs · Cars24 / Spinny-class
12–15%
Fast and flexible — but visibly pricier

The root cause of that gap is a single thing: NBFCs can't read self-employed risk precisely. Faced with uncertainty, a lender protects itself in two costly ways — it rejects what it can't read (lost customers) and adds a fat uncertainty premium (high rate). One blind spot, two losses.

Our intervention
Read risk precisely — cash-flow × collateral
Lever 1 · Out-approve

Approve more → market share

Confidently assess borrowers competitors reject (thin-file, lumpy self-employed). Every safe "yes" others say "no" to is share captured.

Lever 2 · Out-price

Price lower → close the gap

Precise risk reading strips the uncertainty premium. A good borrower gets a rate that moves toward the bank benchmark — the ₹3,000 shrinks.

Both levers come from one engine. We're not picking between volume and margin — reading risk better moves both in the same direction.

Why B2C wins
Market size

B2C is the mass individual-buyer market; B2B is a finite set of known dealers. The share worth capturing lives in B2C.

AI leverage

B2C buyers are heterogeneous and thin-file — exactly where profiling AI is decisive. Dealers are already underwritten on their Cars24 transaction history, so AI adds little.

Where the lacuna lives

The rate-gap pain and the approve/reject pain are individual-buyer problems. B2B credit is a revolving line, not a rate-shopped purchase.

Engine fit

The composite inputs — a person's cash flow × the specific car's value — map to a B2C buyer, not a dealer's working-capital line.

05

The borrower we're designing for

Rajesh is creditworthy but document-poor. His real repayment capacity is hard to see through traditional documents — so he gets rejected, or priced as if he were risky.

Rajesh, 38
Wholesale auto-parts shop · Surat · 6 years in business
Gross receipts~₹45 L / yr
ITR net profit~₹9 L (after expenses)
Real monthly cash₹1.2–1.5 L · lumpy
Credit score690 · thin file
Wants₹6.5 L used SUV (via Cars24)

Why he's hard to underwrite — all of it legitimate

1

No salary slip, no clean monthly credit. Lumpy, seasonal income that salary-template models choke on.

2

The ITR lags reality. An annual snapshot, net of depreciation and real expenses — it understates the cash he actually has today.

3

A thin bureau file. A short formal-credit history at 690 makes him look riskier than he is.

ResultRead as marginal → rejected, under-financed, or quoted a flat high "self-employed" rate. The reality: a strong borrower the document flow can't perceive — so he's a lost customer and, if approved, an over-charged one. Exactly the two losses we are trying to prevent.

07

The engine, and why it compounds

Three components make one "Super-Approval" engine. Together they pull both levers — and the levers multiply.

The market-share formula
Captured volume V = N × a(R) × c(Δr) × t
N — addressable self-employed B2C buyers (large, and under-served)
a(R) — approval rate, rising with risk-read precision R
c(Δr) — offer acceptance, rising as the rate gap Δr shrinks
t — average ticket size
Improving the engine's precision R lifts a(R) (we approve more) and shrinks Δr, which lifts c(Δr) (more approved offers get taken). One investment moves two multiplicative terms — that's why it compounds, and why it beats any single-lever play.
Illustrative — what the two levers do to V
Baseline: 100 self-employed applicants, 45% approved, 55% of offers accepted≈ 25 loans
Lever 1 — better profiling lifts approval 45% → 65%→ 36 loans
Lever 2 — rate gap narrows, acceptance 55% → 72%→ 47 loans
Combined effect on captured volume≈ 1.9×
Numbers illustrative — they show the multiplicative mechanism, not a forecast. Real a(R) and c(Δr) curves are the thing to calibrate next.

Prioritisation, in one line: the highest-leverage thing Loans24 can build is the precision engine R — because in B2C, N is largest and both a and c are most improvable. That is the conclusion the formula forces.
